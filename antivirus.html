Whitelist the site in your antivirus

Some antivirus products block domains at the application level, separate from DNS. If changing DNS didn't help, your antivirus web shield may be the cause.

How to tell if antivirus is the problem

If your browser shows a warning page from your antivirus (not just a generic browser error), that's the clear sign — look for the antivirus name in the warning.

You can also try loading in a private/incognito window. Antivirus web shields usually still apply there, so if it's still blocked, DNS is probably not the issue.

Windows Defender's SmartScreen may show an "Unsafe website" warning you can bypass.

  1. When you see the SmartScreen block, click More information.
  2. Click Go to the site anyway (unsafe).

Browser extension blockers

Many antivirus products also install a browser extension that blocks sites independently. Check chrome://extensions or about:addons for anything from your antivirus vendor and look for an allow-list option within it.

Common culprits: Avast Online Security, AVG Online Security, Kaspersky Protection, Bitdefender TrafficLight.
