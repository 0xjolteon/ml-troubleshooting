What's actually causing this?

The game server sits in Montreal on OVH's network. Depending on your ISP and where you are, the network path between you and that server may take inefficient routes - extra hops, congested links, or high-latency segments that cause packet loss and disconnections.

This is most commonly reported by players in SEA, but it's not exclusive to any region - it comes down to how your specific ISP routes traffic toward eastern Canada. Some ISPs route it cleanly, others don't.

Will WARP definitely help? Not necessarily. WARP reroutes your traffic through Cloudflare's network, which often has better peering toward OVH Montreal - but if your existing path is already good, adding WARP can introduce slightly more latency. It's worth testing: connect WARP and play for a session to see if disconnects improve. If things feel worse, disconnect it.

Step 1 - Test WARP

The quickest way to find out if WARP helps your route is to install it, connect it, and play for a session. If disconnects improve noticeably, keep using it. If latency gets worse or disconnects don't change, turn it off - it's not helping your path.

For the simplest setup (all traffic through WARP, no configuration needed): → Basic WARP setup guide

If you want only game/site traffic to go through WARP and leave everything else (streaming, Discord, other games) on your normal connection: → Zero Trust split tunneling guide

The split tunnel setup is more work to configure but is the better long-term option if you're going to leave WARP running all the time. It keeps your other traffic unaffected and avoids potential issues with other games or services that dislike VPN traffic.

Step 2 - Run a traceroute (if WARP doesn't help)

A traceroute shows the exact path your packets take to the server and where they're getting dropped or delayed. This is one of the most useful pieces of information you can give (in my opinion) when reporting a disconnect issue. 

tracert

Run this in Command Prompt and copy the full output.

traceroute

Run this in Terminal and copy the full output. If it hangs, try traceroute -I instead.

traceroute 
# or if not installed:
mtr --report

mtr gives a more detailed live view and is often more useful than a single traceroute. Post the full output.

When posting your traceroute in Discord, also mention: your ISP name, your country/region, and whether WARP made things better, worse, or no difference. This helps narrow down whether it's a routing issue that has been seen before.

Peak hour congestion

If disconnects only happen during certain times of day - typically evenings in your local timezone - that's usually ISP congestion rather than a routing problem. Your ISP's links become saturated during peak hours and start dropping packets.

WARP can sometimes help here too, as it may route around congested ISP segments. But if congestion is severe enough, it's possible neither WARP nor any other fix on your end will fully solve it - it's a capacity issue on your ISP's network.
