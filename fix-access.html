Problem 1 — Site Access

Can't reach
the site?

The domain gets blocked by many ISPs and antivirus products due to the TLD's reputation. There are several ways to get around it — start with the flowchart for your OS, or jump to a specific fix below.

Three main fixes, in order of ease: changing your DNS to bypass your ISP's blocklist, whitelisting the site in your antivirus, or using Cloudflare WARP to encrypt your traffic entirely so it can't be inspected and blocked. Firefox users also have a quick built-in option via DNS over HTTPS.

Fix 1 — Change your DNS

Your ISP's DNS resolver may be blocking the domain. Switching to Cloudflare's public resolver (1.1.1.1) bypasses your ISP's blocklist. This is the fastest fix and doesn't change anything else about how your internet works.

→ Full instructions for all three OSes: DNS Fix guide

Fix 2 — DNS over HTTPS in Firefox

If you use Firefox, you can enable DNS over HTTPS (DoH) without changing any system settings. This encrypts your DNS lookups and routes them through Cloudflare, bypassing ISP-level filtering.

  1. Open Firefox and go to Settings → Privacy & Security.
  2. Scroll down to DNS over HTTPS.
  3. Select Max Protection and choose Cloudflare as the provider.
  4. Reload the page.
This only applies to Firefox. Other browsers (Chrome, Edge) have their own DoH settings — look for "Secure DNS" or "Use secure DNS" in their privacy settings, with Cloudflare (1.1.1.1) as the provider.

Fix 3 — Whitelist in your antivirus

If your antivirus web shield is blocking the site — you'll usually see a branded warning page rather than a browser error — you need to add an exception for the domain.

→ Step-by-step for Avast, AVG, Malwarebytes, Norton, Kaspersky, Bitdefender: Antivirus guide

Norton users: Norton's web protection operates at the network driver level and can block traffic even when DNS is changed. Whitelisting in Norton is the right fix, but if it doesn't work, WARP (Fix 4) is the reliable fallback since it encrypts traffic before Norton can inspect it.

Fix 4 — Cloudflare WARP (last resort)

If DNS changes and antivirus whitelisting haven't worked, your ISP may be performing deep packet inspection — blocking the connection at the traffic level, not just the DNS level. Cloudflare WARP encrypts your traffic end-to-end, which prevents this kind of inspection.

WARP is free and takes about 5 minutes to set up. Once connected, it also uses Cloudflare's DNS (1.1.1.1) automatically, so it replaces Fix 1 as well.
