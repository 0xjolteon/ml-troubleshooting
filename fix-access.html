Problem 1 - Site Access

Can't reach
the site?

The domain gets blocked by many ISPs and antivirus products due to the TLD's reputation. There are several ways to get around it - start with the flowchart for your OS, or jump to a specific fix below.

Three main fixes, in order of ease: changing your DNS to bypass your ISP's blocklist, whitelisting the site in your antivirus, or using Cloudflare WARP (or a commercial VPN) to encrypt your traffic entirely so it can't be inspected and blocked. Firefox users also have a quick built-in option via DNS over HTTPS.

Fix 1 - Change your DNS

Your ISP's DNS resolver may be blocking the domain. Switching to Cloudflare's public resolvers (1.1.1.1 and 1.0.0.1) bypasses your ISP's blocklist. This is the fastest fix and doesn't change anything else about how your internet works.

→ Full instructions for all three OSes: DNS Fix guide

Fix 2 - DNS over HTTPS in Firefox

If you use Firefox, you can enable DNS over HTTPS (DoH) without changing any system settings. This encrypts your DNS lookups and routes them through Cloudflare, bypassing ISP-level filtering.

  1. Open Firefox and go to Settings → Privacy & Security.
  2. Scroll down to DNS over HTTPS.
  3. Select Max Protection and choose Cloudflare as the provider.
  4. Reload the page.
This only applies to Firefox. Other browsers (Chrome, Edge) have their own DoH settings - look for "Secure DNS" or "Use secure DNS" in their privacy settings, with Cloudflare ( 1.1.1.1) as the provider.

Fix 3 - Whitelist in your antivirus

If your antivirus web shield is blocking the site - you'll usually see a branded warning page rather than a browser error - you need to add an exception for the domain.

→ Step-by-step for Avast, AVG, Malwarebytes, Norton, Kaspersky, Bitdefender: Antivirus guide

Norton users: Norton's web protection operates at the network driver level and can block traffic even when DNS is changed. Whitelisting in Norton is the right fix, but if it doesn't work, WARP (Fix 4) is the reliable fallback since it encrypts traffic before Norton can inspect it.

Fix 4 - Use a VPN you already have

If you already subscribe to a paid VPN (Mullvad, ProtonVPN, ExpressVPN, NordVPN, etc.), connecting it is the fastest path from here - it encrypts your traffic end-to-end, which bypasses both ISP DNS blocking and deep packet inspection in one step.

Just connect your VPN and try loading the site. If it works, you're done - no further configuration needed.

Free VPNs are not recommended. Free VPN services typically have bandwidth caps, slow speeds, unreliable uptime, and in many cases fund themselves by logging and selling your traffic data - the opposite of what a VPN is supposed to do. If you don't have a paid VPN, use Cloudflare WARP (Fix 5) instead - it's genuinely free, operated by a reputable company, and purpose-built for this kind of use.

Fix 5 - Cloudflare WARP (free alternative to a VPN)

If you don't have a paid VPN, or if fixes 1-3 haven't worked, Cloudflare WARP is the recommended free option. It encrypts your traffic end-to-end - blocking deep packet inspection - and automatically uses Cloudflare's DNS (1.1.1.1), so it replaces Fix 1 as well.

WARP is free, maintained by Cloudflare (a major internet infrastructure company), and takes about 5 minutes to set up. It is not a traditional VPN - it doesn't hide your IP or route through servers in other countries for Netflix - but for the purpose of bypassing a domain block, it works just as well.
